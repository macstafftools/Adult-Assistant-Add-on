Module 1

The Adult Journey

Understanding why adults are here — and what they expect — changes how you support the team.

Adult customers are excited and invested in their journey. They expect professionalism, organization, and added value. They are more attuned to instructor behaviour than any other group at MacSailing.

🟢 Your role in the journey

As an Assistant Instructor, you support the Lead. You're not running the class — but you are always visible, always active, and always part of the experience. Adults notice when you're engaged. They also notice when you're not.

CANSail Levels & Fleet

Know the levels and boats so you can coach confidently and answer student questions accurately.

1
CANSail 1

Goal: get students comfortable and able to sail to any point on the water.

  • Sailing to any point of sail
  • Righting a capsized boat
  • Basic knots and right of way
  • Completion: can rent a Zest in light/moderate winds
2
CANSail 2

Goal: sail efficiently to any point. More focus on sail trim and boat speed.

  • Proper sail trim and boat speed
  • Close-hauled to close-hauled tacks
  • Completion: can rent a 420 in moderate winds
3
CANSail 3

Big step up — advanced boat handling and introduction to racing.

  • Boat speed through sail control adjustments
  • Advanced maneuvers — steering with heel, backwards sailing
  • Introduction to racing skills

Module 3

Teaching Adults

Adults are paying and they're aware. Your coaching style needs to match that.

You teach the same material as youth programs but the style changes completely. Adults are paying for their own courses and they notice everything — including when you're not fully present.

What Changes

💬
Explain the Why

Connect ideas to daily life. "We look before we tack just like you shoulder check before turning." Adults need context to learn, not just instruction.

🎯
Constant Feedback

Be active on the water — always. Adults notice when you go quiet. Watch faces for confusion and respond before they disengage.

💪
Be Confident

Highlight your experience during introductions. Adults trust confidence. If you're uncertain, check with the Lead rather than guessing.

❤️
Be Patient

Everyone learns at different speeds. Remind students of this. Your patience creates the safe environment where adults are willing to try and fail.

🎓
Your teaching opportunity: If you feel confident teaching a specific lesson, talk to your Lead Instructor about running it. Each assistant should get a chance to teach.

When You're Not Teaching

  • Stay engaged in the lesson — watch, listen, be ready
  • Watch students' facial expressions to gauge understanding or confusion
  • Provide on-water feedback constantly — you are an extension of the Lead
  • Flag anything you notice to the Lead after the session

Module 5

Your Role on the Team

You support the Lead. That means being proactive, prepared, and fully present — every session.

Schedule

FormatSchedule
Weekday evenings5 days in a row · 6–9pm (shifts later in season)
WeekendsTwo weekends in a row · mornings or evenings
Arrive20 minutes before class start — talking to students and answering questions

Before the Session Starts

  • Get coach boats, wetsuits, and wetsuit washing station ready on arrival
  • Be in the classroom 20 minutes early — talking to students, answering questions
  • Check the office for consent forms unless office staff are present
  • Check in with the Lead — know the plan before class starts

On the Water

  • Check each boat's rigging before launching — make sure the plugs are in
  • Ensure equal sailing time for all participants
  • Keep track of who is launching first each day — vary the order
  • Keep track of who is skippering vs crewing more — balance it
  • Provide constant, specific feedback to students on the water

CANSail Logs

  • Sit down as a group at the end of every class to complete the log
  • Covers student issues and who needs extra help next session
  • Log any boat repairs — QR code for the CANSail log is on the office cupboard door
  • First log: Sail Canada registration · Last log: makeup classes
⚠️
Simply being on site is not working. Check in with your Lead, interact with the class, and stay engaged. If you're unsure what to do, ask the Lead directly.

Module 6

Wind & Conditions

Know how conditions change the session so you can support the Lead and coach students effectively.

Wind Level Focus Points

🍃
Light Wind
  • Flat boat
  • Smooth movements to keep momentum
  • Great for beginners building confidence
💨
Medium Wind
  • Crew doesn't cross the middle line until the skipper does
  • Good for developing skill and boat speed
🌬️
Heavy Wind
  • Sail powers up until correctly trimmed — think about balance position
  • If necessary — drop the sheet, never drop the tiller

Light Wind Day — Your Role

  • Support the rig and safety check at the start
  • Launch for any breeze at all (1–3 knots) — steering and balance practice
  • On land: help run knots, right-of-way scenarios, rigging challenges alongside the Lead
  • Keep morale positive — frame it as real-world sailing awareness, not a cancelled session
Module 7

Scenarios

Situations you'll face as an Assistant. Read each one, think it through, then reveal the answer.

Scenario 1 — Student Frustration

Coaching ▼

"An adult learner becomes frustrated after struggling with a skill. How do you support them without undermining the Lead?"

"Everyone is learning — these are completely new skills and they take time." Break the skill down: identify what they're doing well and what's causing trouble. Stay calm — your patience is contagious. If the student needs more support than you can give on the water, flag it to your Lead after the session. The Lead can take over the conversation if needed.

Scenario 2 — Mobility Challenges

Inclusion ▼

"A student is struggling to cross the boat — mobility issues are affecting their ability to tack. This comes up more often in Zests than 420s."

Ask if they're okay or experiencing discomfort — they probably already know what's wrong. Find modifications: placing the back foot across before the tack, adjusting tiller exchange timing. Watch them demonstrate another tack to see exactly what's causing trouble. Mention other options — 420s next course, cruising as an alternative. For anything significant, make sure it's communicated to the Lead and a supervisor.

Scenario 3 — Light Wind Day

Conditions ▼

"It's nearly windless. The Lead is running a land session. How do you support it?"

Help run the stations alongside the Lead — knots, right-of-way scenarios, rigging challenges, dryland tacks. Stay engaged and enthusiastic. Keep morale positive: "This is part of sailing — you're building real awareness." Take the lead on specific stations if the Lead asks you to. Never be passive during a land session.
